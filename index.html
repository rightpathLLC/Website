Claims We Handle

Property damage may involve more than the visible repair cost. Depending on the policy and facts of the loss, claims may include building damage, contents, equipment, inventory, business income, and additional expenses.

Residential Claims

  • Water damage and pipe burst
  • Fire and smoke damage
  • Storm, wind, hail and tree impact
  • Theft and vandalism
  • Rental and condo claims
  • Additional living expense review

Commercial Claims

  • Commercial water and fire losses
  • Restaurant, retail, office and warehouse claims
  • Tenant improvements
  • Inventory and contents damage
  • Multi-family and rental properties
  • Business income and extra expense

Equipment & Business Losses

  • Equipment breakdown
  • Mechanical and electrical losses
  • HVAC damage
  • Refrigeration failure
  • Machinery-related losses
  • Operational disruption review

Commercial Claims Require a Different Approach

For business owners, a loss is rarely just a repair issue. It can affect income, operations, employees, customers, inventory, equipment, and lease responsibilities.

Business Owners

We assist restaurants, dry cleaners, salons, retail stores, liquor stores, warehouses, offices, car wash facilities, and other business operations.

Property Owners & Landlords

We assist commercial property owners, multi-family property owners, rental property owners, and landlords when property damage affects buildings or tenants.

Business Income Review

When operations are interrupted, business income and extra expense issues may need careful documentation and review.

Why Work With a Public Adjuster?

Insurance company adjusters review claims for the insurance carrier. A Public Adjuster works for the policyholder.

Policy Review
We review coverage, conditions, limitations, and claim-related requirements.
Damage Assessment
We help identify the full scope of damages that may need to be documented.
Claim Presentation
We organize the claim so the loss is presented clearly and professionally.
Carrier Communication
We communicate with the insurance company and help move the claim forward.

Before You Accept the Insurance Company’s Decision

If you received a low estimate, partial payment, denial, repeated document requests, or confusing claim decision, a claim review may help you understand your options.

Frequently Asked Questions

When should I contact a Public Adjuster?

Ideally before filing the claim or before the insurance company’s inspection. You may also contact us after receiving a low estimate, partial payment, denial, or confusing decision.

Can you review an old claim?

Depending on the policy, facts, timing, and documentation, a supplemental review may be possible. Contact us before assuming the claim is fully closed.

Do you handle commercial claims?

Yes. We handle residential and commercial claims, including business interruption, equipment breakdown, inventory, and building damage issues.
