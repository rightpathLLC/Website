Why Right Path

We work to help you understand what your policy provides.

Insurance companies review claims from their own perspective. A licensed Public Adjuster works for the policyholder and helps organize the claim, review the policy, assess the damage, and communicate with the insurance carrier.

Our role is to reduce confusion, identify potential claim issues, and help the policyholder present the loss clearly and professionally.

Before accepting the insurance company's decision, consider a claim review.

A first estimate, partial payment, or coverage letter may not always reflect the full claim picture. If the payment seems low, the claim was denied, or the process is confusing, a review may help clarify your options.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do you handle commercial insurance claims?

Yes. We assist with commercial property damage, equipment-related losses, business interruption, inventory damage, and other claim-related issues.

Can you review a claim that already received payment?

Often, yes. Depending on the policy, documentation, timing, and facts of the loss, a supplemental review may be possible.

When should I contact a Public Adjuster?

Ideally before filing the claim or before the insurance company's inspection. You may also contact us after receiving a low estimate, denial, or confusing claim decision.

How do Public Adjusters charge?

Public Adjusters typically charge a percentage of the claim recovery. The consultation is free, and the fee structure is explained before engagement.
